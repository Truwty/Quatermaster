Orders of Dress

Complete specifications for all British Army ceremonial and service uniforms.

Dress Order Specifications

The British Army maintains strict standards for all orders of dress. Each dress order has specific requirements for uniform components, accessories, and occasions of wear.

Full Dress

Who Can Wear It

  • Household Cavalry and Royal Horse Artillery.
  • Certain ceremonial units by tradition.

When It Is Worn

  • State occasions and major ceremonial events.
  • Royal ceremonies and parades.

Uniform Details

  • Coat: Red tunic with gold braiding and regimental facings.
  • Trousers: Blue overalls with gold stripe.
  • Headwear: Bearskin cap or busby.
  • Footwear: Black riding boots.
  • Accessories: White crossbelt, pouch, and sword.

Regimental Distinctions

  • Regimental buttons, plumes, and cap badges.
  • Distinctive regimental colors and patterns.

Frock Coat

Who Can Wear It

  • Officers of certain regiments and corps.
  • Used by some regimental bands and ceremonial units.

When It Is Worn

  • Formal ceremonies and parades.
  • Regimental dinners and formal occasions.

Uniform Details

  • Coat: Dark blue frock coat with gold braiding.
  • Trousers: Dark blue with gold stripe.
  • Headwear: Shako or bearskin.
  • Footwear: Black boots.
  • Accessories: White gloves, sash, and sword.

Regimental Distinctions

  • Regimental collar badges and buttons.
  • Distinctive gold lace patterns.

No.1 Dress

Who Can Wear It

  • All ranks for ceremonial occasions.
  • Most commonly worn dress uniform.

When It Is Worn

  • Parades, ceremonies, and formal occasions.
  • Church services and state functions.
  • Guard duties and public events.

Uniform Details

  • Coat: Red tunic with brass buttons and regimental facings.
  • Trousers: Dark blue with red stripe.
  • Headwear: peaked cap or bearskin.
  • Footwear: Black boots or shoes.
  • Accessories: White belt, gloves, and medals.

Regimental Distinctions

  • Regimental collar badges and shoulder titles.
  • Distinctive regimental buttons and colors.
  • Regimental stable belts and lanyards.

No.2 Dress

Who Can Wear It

  • All ranks for semi-formal occasions.
  • Used when No.1 Dress is not required.

When It Is Worn

  • Less formal parades and ceremonies.
  • Official visits and receptions.
  • Mess functions and social events.

Uniform Details

  • Coat: Khaki service dress jacket.
  • Trousers: Khaki service dress trousers.
  • Headwear: Service dress cap.
  • Footwear: Black boots or shoes.
  • Accessories: Brown leather belt and medals.

Regimental Distinctions

  • Regimental collar badges and buttons.
  • Regimental shoulder flashes.

No.3 Dress

Who Can Wear It

  • All headquarters & Royal Military Police members of the rank Brigadier and above, for walking out and off-duty wear.
  • Informal service dress.

When It Is Worn

  • Walking out in barracks and local areas.
  • Informal duties and off-duty occasions.
  • Travel and administrative duties.

Uniform Details

  • Coat: Open-necked bush jacket.
  • Trousers: Khaki drill trousers.
  • Headwear: Optional field service cap.
  • Footwear: Brown boots or shoes.
  • Accessories: Brown leather belt.

Regimental Distinctions

  • Regimental collar badges.
  • Regimental shoulder titles.

No.4 Dress

Who Can Wear It

  • All Headquarters ranks & British Army General Officers for field and combat operations.
  • Standard combat uniform.

When It Is Worn

  • Field operations and combat duties.
  • Training exercises and deployments.
  • General duties in operational areas.

Uniform Details

  • Coat: Combat jacket with camouflage pattern.
  • Trousers: Combat trousers with camouflage pattern.
  • Headwear: Combat helmet or beret.
  • Footwear: Combat boots.
  • Accessories: Webbing, body armor, and equipment.

Regimental Distinctions

  • Regimental shoulder flashes and arm badges.
  • Formation signs and qualification badges.

No.8 Dress

Who Can Wear It

  • All ranks for formal evening occasions.
  • White tie equivalent.

When It Is Worn

  • Formal dinners and balls.
  • State banquets and royal occasions.
  • Regimental mess functions.

Uniform Details

  • Coat: Black tailcoat with silk facings.
  • Trousers: Black trousers with silk stripe.
  • Shirt: White dress shirt with wing collar.
  • Tie: White bow tie.
  • Footwear: Black patent leather shoes.

Regimental Distinctions

  • Regimental buttons and cuff links.
  • Regimental mess kit variations.

No.13 Dress

Who Can Wear It

  • All ranks for tropical climates.
  • Used in hot weather conditions.

When It Is Worn

  • Operations in tropical and desert climates.
  • Training in hot weather conditions.

Uniform Details

  • Coat: Short-sleeved bush jacket.
  • Trousers: Short trousers.
  • Headwear: Tropical helmet or bush hat.
  • Footwear: Brown boots or sandals.
  • Accessories: Tropical webbing and equipment.

Regimental Distinctions

  • Regimental collar badges and shoulder flashes.
  • Tropical regimental variations.

No.14 Dress

Who Can Wear It

  • All ranks for extreme cold weather.
  • Arctic and mountain operations.

When It Is Worn

  • Operations in arctic and cold climates.
  • Mountain and winter warfare.

Uniform Details

  • Coat: White arctic camouflage jacket.
  • Trousers: White arctic camouflage trousers.
  • Headwear: White arctic hood or cap.
  • Footwear: White arctic boots.
  • Accessories: White arctic webbing and equipment.

Regimental Distinctions

  • Arctic regimental arm badges.
  • Special forces variations available.

AAC Flightsuit

Who Can Wear It

  • Army Air Corps personnel.
  • Aircrew and aviation support staff.

When It Is Worn

  • Flight operations and aviation duties.
  • Aviation training and maintenance.

Uniform Details

  • Suit: Green flightsuit with zip front.
  • Headwear: Flying helmet with communications.
  • Footwear: Flying boots.
  • Accessories: Life preserver, harness, and survival equipment.

Regimental Distinctions

  • AAC wings and qualification badges.
  • Rank insignia on flightsuit.
